Places to visit in Antalya

  • Düden Waterfall
  • Aspendos Ancient City
  • Yivliminare Mosque
  • Damlataş Cave
  • Hadrian's Gate
  • Kurşunlu Waterfall
  • Alanya Castle

1) Düden Waterfall

Since Düden Waterfall is fed especially by underground sources, it has a very intense water flow, regardless of summer and winter months.

Location

2) Aspendos Ancient City

The theater here was built in M.S. It was built by the Romans in the 2nd century. The city was built on two hills, one large and one small.

Location

3) Yivliminare Mosque

Yivli Minare is one of the first Islamic buildings in Antalya. It is a Seljuk work from the 13th century.

Location

4) Damlataş Cave

In addition to its fascinating beauty, the cave is also famous for its air that is good for asthma patients. There are patients who sit in the cave for a certain period of time under the supervision of a doctor and undergo a 21-day treatment cure.

Location

5) Hadrian's Gate

Hadrian's Gate or Üçkapılar is a monumental triumphal arch built on the city walls surrounding the city, in honor of the visit of Roman Emperor Hadrian to Antalya in 130. It consists of three arched gates with four marble columns on each side and two towers next to them.

Location

6) Kurşunlu Waterfall

The waterfall, located in a 2-kilometer canyon where seven small ponds are connected to each other with small waterfalls, was opened to visitors in 1986.

Location

7) Alanya Castle

Alanya Castle is one of the symbols of Alanya, the district of Antalya. It is located on a peninsula rising approximately 250 meters above the sea. The total length of its walls is 6.5 kilometers. The castle was built in the Hellenistic period in the Alanya settlement, which was called Kandeleri in ancient times.

Location

8) Kızılkule

Kızılkule, a unique example of 13th century medieval Mediterranean defense structures; It was built by the Seljuk ruler Alaaddin Keykubat I in order to protect the port, shipyard and Alanya Castle against attacks from the sea.

Location
